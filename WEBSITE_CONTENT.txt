ABOUT / INTRODUCTORY STATEMENT
================================================================================

I am a plant ecologist interested in how climate change affects plants over time, from individuals and populations to entire communities. My scientific motivation is to understand the processes driving plant responses in order to improve forecasts of biodiversity across time and space.

================================================================================
RESEARCH PROGRAM HEADER
================================================================================

"From processes to predictions across scales"

================================================================================
RESEARCH PROGRAM DESCRIPTION
================================================================================

My research combines field experiments, observational data, and statistical modeling to understand how ecological change unfolds over time. I ask how response rates and lags shape the predictability of future biodiversity change.

================================================================================
RESEARCH THEMES (Three Core Areas)
================================================================================

1. POPULATIONS & COMMUNITIES
I examine how plant populations and communities respond to climate change, from individual performance and population dynamics to shifts in community composition across environmental gradients.

2. PROCESSES OVER TIME
I focus on the temporal processes that shape ecological change, including rates of adjustment, delayed responses, and transient dynamics, as well as the roles of acclimation and biotic interactions.

3. PREDICTABILITY & FORECASTING
A central goal of my work is to understand when ecological change is predictable, when it is not, and how response lags limit our ability to forecast future biodiversity patterns.

================================================================================
KEY STATEMENT / RESEARCH PHILOSOPHY
================================================================================

"Ecological change often unfolds more slowly than climate change itself."

My research shows that populations and communities can remain in long transient states, where ecological reorganization lags behind climate change. Accounting for these lags is essential for improving forecasts of future biodiversity.

================================================================================
COLLABORATIONS STATEMENT

Many of the questions I work on—about rates of change, delayed responses, and predictability—can only be addressed through collaboration. RESEARCH PROJECTS
================================================================================

PROJECT 1: RATES AND LAGS OF PLANT RESPONSES TO CLIMATE CHANGE
---------------------------------------------------------------------------
Tags: Whole-community transplants

Description:
Using whole-community transplant experiments, this project studies the pace of plant responses to warming and drought. It focuses on delayed and transient dynamics across populations, communities, and ecosystem processes, and their consequences for predicting biodiversity change.

PROJECT 2: PLANT ACCLIMATION AND RANGE SHIFTS UNDER CLIMATE CHANGE
---------------------------------------------------------------------------
Tags: Trait plasticity · Range shifts

Description:
This project examines whether in situ plant responses, quantified through trait change in historical herbarium specimens, help explain mismatches between predicted and observed range shifts under climate change.

PROJECT 3: PROCESSES SHAPING SPECIES RANGES AND COMMUNITY ASSEMBLY ALONG AN ELEVATIONAL GRADIENT
---------------------------------------------------------------------------
Tags: Community assembly · Species ranges

Description:
This project disentangles environmental, spatial, and biotic contributions to species ranges and community assembly along an elevational gradient, clarifying which aspects of plant ranges and community assembly are potentially structured by deterministic processes.

PROJECT 4: CLIMATE–VEGETATION DISEQUILIBRIUM ACROSS THE HOLOCENE AND ANTHROPOCENE
---------------------------------------------------------------------------
Tags: Climate–vegetation disequilibrium

Description:
This project compares rates of climate and vegetation change across the Holocene and Anthropocene to quantify climate–vegetation disequilibrium under accelerating climate change.

PROJECT 5: IMPROVING ECOLOGICAL FORECASTING
---------------------------------------------------------------------------
Tags: Ecological forecasting

Description:
This project develops and evaluates near-term ecological forecasts across systems, integrating data, models, and evaluation to understand when and why ecological change is predictable.

INTERACTIVE SCIENCE APPLICATIONS
================================================================================

APPLICATION 1: TRANSPLANT NETWORK EXPLORER
---------------------------------------------------------------------------
Tags: Community transplants · Climate change

Description:
Explore whole-community transplant experiments conducted across elevational gradients. The app allows users to examine how plant communities respond to experimental warming, compare response rates across sites, and visualize temporal dynamics in community composition.

Link: https://billurbektas.shinyapps.io/transplantshiny-1/

APPLICATION 2: DOÑANA VEGETATION FORECASTING CHALLENGE
---------------------------------------------------------------------------
Tags: Ecological forecasting · Vegetation dynamics

Description:
Interactive platform developed within the Doñana Digital Twin to explore and submit near-term forecasts of vegetation dynamics. The application presents forecast targets, model outputs, and evaluation metrics, enabling comparison of alternative forecasting approaches and transparent assessment of predictability.

Link: https://donanadt.lifewatch.dev/app/forecasting-vegetation-dynamics

APPLICATION 3: EUROPEAN ECOLOGICAL FORECASTING APPLICATIONS DATABASE
---------------------------------------------------------------------------
Tags: Ecological forecasting · Open infrastructure

Description:
Curated database documenting ecological forecasting efforts across Europe. The platform collects and standardizes metadata on forecast targets, data sources, modeling approaches, and evaluation practices, providing an overview of the current forecasting landscape and its future potential. The application accepts contributions of forecasting application metadata to expand the network.

Link: https://billurbektas.shinyapps.io/eu_forecasting_applications/
